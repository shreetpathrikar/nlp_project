    12. frequency distribution

    In Natural Language Processing (NLP), a frequency distribution is a way of representing the number of times each word or phrase appears in a corpus of text. It is a list of the words or phrases in the corpus, along with the number of times each one appears. The FreqDist class in the NLTK library is used to create and manipulate frequency distributions. It takes a list of words or phrases as input and creates a dictionary where the keys are the words or phrases and the values are the number of times they appear.
